"Free" & "Open" ???

"Free" does not essentially mean 'free of cost' ,

it refers to the 'freedom' a user gets :

  • Freedom of using the product
  • Freedom to look into it's code/content
  • Freedom to modify it's code/content
  • Freedom to re-distribute the work

There is nothing wrong in charging money for a software/program.

"Free" here should be considered as used in 'free speech' , not 'free food'.

"Open" refers to the transperancy in the execution of the program.

It means you have the freedom to look into what happens behind the scenes.

The user decides whether to allow specific details or vice-versa.
